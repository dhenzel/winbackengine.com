## Audience

Marketing and operations leaders at multi-location service businesses — dental
DSOs, fitness franchises, medspa chains, beauty/salon brands, pet services,
home services — who have lapsed customers sitting in their CRM and no
dedicated reactivation motion to recover them.

## Voice

- Direct, numbers-first, skeptical of hype. This reader has seen every
  "AI will revolutionize your business" pitch and tunes out.
- Lead with a concrete scenario or a specific number, not a platitude.
- Short paragraphs. Active voice. Don't pad sentences.
- Assume the reader knows their industry — don't over-explain basics.
- No em-dashes as rhythm crutch. No "in today's fast-paced world." No "leverage."
  No "unlock." No "game-changing." No "revolutionize."

## Claims the brand stands behind (safe to reuse)

- Trained human agents call lapsed customers and get them to rebook.
  (Not bots, not AI voice agents, not scripts on autopilot.)
- 3x ROI guaranteed on reactivation campaigns.
- We typically recover 25–40% of lapsed customers contacted by phone.
- Powered by LTVplus — a 500+ person global customer support operation.

## Structure

- Open with a specific scenario (2–3 sentences) or a specific dollar figure.
- Include at least one table or numbered breakdown when discussing economics.
- Link to the relevant pillar page when introducing a concept covered there.
- End with a CTA to the booking widget (not /audit, not /roi-calculator —
  use the full booking URL: https://api.leadconnectorhq.com/widget/booking/1Be1vCXeKrBtUIrx4WBb).

## What NOT to write

- Generic "5 tips for customer retention" blog fluff.
- Anything that sounds like every other AI-generated SEO post.
- Inflated stats without a source, or vague claims ("studies show...").
